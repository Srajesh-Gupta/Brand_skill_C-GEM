# C-GEM Voice & Tone Reference

## Core register

Declarative, mission-driven, earnest. Grounded practitioner with policy-research rigour embedded in raw field experience. Direct, authoritative, sober. C-GEM's copy never reads like marketing ‚ÄĒ it reads like an organisation that has taken a moral position.

The reader should come away thinking: "This organisation knows what it's talking about and has earned the right to say it."

---

## The six content rules

### 1. First-person plural
"We sit between farming communities and the market." "We take no stake in the credits."
‚ÄĒ Never "I". Rarely "you" (reserved for direct calls to action).

### 2. Stewards, not beneficiaries
Farmers are *stewards* ‚ÄĒ protagonists of every artefact. They *thrive, lead, negotiate, own, protect*.
‚ÄĒ Never: *receive, qualify, access, benefit from*. The word "beneficiary/beneficiaries" is banned.
‚ÄĒ Communities are referred to as "smallholder farmers" or "communities" ‚ÄĒ never "beneficiaries".

### 3. Indian numerals, always
`2,71,000 farming families` ‚ÄĒ never `271,000`. This is non-negotiable.
Lakh/crore for domestic contexts: `27 lakh` not `2.7 million` when addressing Indian audiences.

### 4. No hype
**Banned words:** revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, unlock the future, transformative, powerful, leverage (as buzzword), empowerment-as-marketing.
**On-brand alternatives:** fair, transparent, accountable, verifiable, rooted, rights-based, community-led, rigorous, traceable, independent.

### 5. No emoji. Anywhere.
Use real iconography (Lucide stroke icons), SDG tiles, mono labels, or nothing. This includes ūüĆĪ ūüĆć ūü§Ě ‚ÄĒ replace with a Lucide stroke or ‚ÄĒ usually ‚ÄĒ nothing.

### 6. No vague environmental claims
Every environmental claim needs backing data. "C-GEM-verified projects have sequestered X tonnes" not "helping the planet breathe".

## What C-GEM is and isn't

| C-GEM IS | C-GEM IS NOT |
|----------|--------------|
| Independent accountability partner | Programme developer |
| Contract reviewer & negotiator | Carbon credit buyer |
| Rights-based intermediary | Market implementer |
| Non-profit, community-accountable | Consultancy |

This distinction is the brand's moral position. It must come through in every piece of copy.

## Audience-specific tone adjustments

**Farming communities / FPOs:** Warm, plain language, active voice. Lead with "what this means for you and your land." Avoid jargon.

**CSO / NGO partners:** Collegial, rigorous. Reference shared frameworks (SBTi, Verra, Gold Standard) without needing to explain them. Acknowledge complexity.

**Carbon credit buyers / corporate partners:** Precise, audit-ready language. Transparency and traceability are foregrounded. Avoid warmth-signalling.

**Funders & philanthropists:** Mission-grounded, evidence-led. Outcomes framed as community-owned results, not programme metrics.

**Regulators / policy audiences:** Formal, citation-ready. Data and methodology first. Legal entity name (Eco-Value Empowerment Foundation) surfaces here.
